About

Encode Justice is an International organization fighting for justice in the age of Artificial Intellgience (AI). In Canada, our goal is simple: make AI a political issue.

Growing Importance of AI

Ai has rapidly become a part of daily life. From email spam filters to your daily commute, everyone likely encounters some form of AI technology each day. However, while AI can be used in small and beneficial ways, it can also be harmful.

Facial recognition systems, for example, falsely identify people of oclour at a higher rate than white people - therefore, when used by police departments, facial recognition amplifies racist policing. Furthermore, facial recognition systems rely on large datasets of people's faces and names to "learn" and identify subjects. These datasets can come from anywhere: Clearview AI, for instance, uses (nonconsensually) billions of images posted on social media to provide facial recognition services to police departments across Canada.

The risks of AI are not limited to the technology's uses - rather, the problems start at the root. AI algorithms rely on massive amounts of data to make accurate predictions and continually improve (this is where the "intellgience" comes in), so companies interested in developing or using AI technology are incentivized to collect data from their uses, often without hteir informed consent. This is botha violation of privacy and theft of personal data, and many people do not even know what is happening.

This is where Encode Justice Canada comes in.

Our goal is to ensure that AI serves to benefit people, not companies. We do this in three ways:


  • Teach Canadians about how AI works and how it is used
  • Collaborate with other organizations across Canada to produce and distribute AI educational materials.
  • Reach out to schools and universities across the country to inform youth about AI and how they can help make sure it works for them.

  • Research and analyze policy regulating data and AI in Canada
  • Work with organizations and policymakers on developing policy commentaries and proposals that ensure responsible use of AI across Canada
  • Participate in developing a new understanding of relationship between the public and digital spheres

  • Advocate for the fair and ethical use of AI by corporations and governments alike
  • Advertise and publicize effective tech policy
  • Connect similar orgnization sand initiative to for ma Pan-Canadian network for the pursuit of good AI
